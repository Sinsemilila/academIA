# Bibliographie scientifique AcademIA

Sources consultées pour fonder la taxonomie d'erreur graduée et la pédagogie du Teacher. Organisée par thème. À utiliser comme point d'ancrage des décisions pédagogiques (cf. ADR-002, ADR-003).

---

## 1. Théorie de l'erreur en acquisition L2 (SLA)

### Fondateurs

- **Corder, S. P. (1967)**. "The Significance of Learners' Errors". *IRAL* 5, 161-170. [ERIC ED019903](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=ED019903)
  → distinction **mistake** (performance) vs **error** (compétence). Socle de notre tier T4 `regressive`.

- **Selinker, L. (1972)**. "Interlanguage". *IRAL* 10(3), 209-231.
  → 5 processus : transfert L1, surgénéralisation L2, transfert de training, stratégies com/apprentissage. **Fossilisation**. Fonde notre table L1 transfer multipliers.

- **Krashen, S. (1982)**. *Principles and Practice in Second Language Acquisition*. Pergamon.
  → Input Hypothesis (i+1), **Affective Filter**. Justifie le tier T1 `ignored` — protéger la prise de risque.

- **Long, M. (1996)**. "The role of the linguistic environment in SLA". *Handbook of SLA* (Ritchie & Bhatia).
  → Interaction Hypothesis. Négociation de sens > input passif.

- **Pienemann, M. (1998)**. *Language Processing and Second Language Development: Processability Theory*. Benjamins.
  → **Teachability Hypothesis** : structures non enseignables hors séquence. Fonde le tier T0 `pre_acquisition`.

- **DeKeyser, R. (2007)**. *Practice in a Second Language*. CUP.
  → Skill Acquisition Theory : déclaratif → procédural → automatique. Justifie la distinction mistake vs error selon l'ancienneté d'exposition.

### Error gravity

- **James, C. (1998)**. *Errors in Language Learning and Use*. Longman.
  → **3 axes de gravité** (linguistic, communicative, social/pedagogical). Pilier de notre structure `gravity_axes` en taxonomie v2.

- **Burt, M. & Kiparsky, C. (1972)**. *The Gooficon*.
  → **Global vs local errors**. Global = nuit à la compréhension, penalized partout ; local = dégradé par niveau.

- **Khalil, A. (1985)**. "Communicative Error Evaluation: Native Speakers' Evaluation and Interpretation of Written Errors of Arab EFL Learners". *TESOL Quarterly* 19, 335-351.
  → Erreurs lexicales nuisent plus à la compréhension que grammaticales à poids équivalent.

- **Rifkin, B. & Roberts, F. (1995)**. "Error Gravity: A Critical Review of Research Design". *Language Learning* 45(3), 511-537.
  → **Méta-critique** des hiérarchies d'error gravity publiées : données souvent non généralisables. Justifie la calibration empirique (pas copier une hiérarchie).

### Feedback correctif

- **Lyster, R. & Ranta, L. (1997)**. "Corrective feedback and learner uptake". *SSLA* 19(1), 37-66. [DOI 10.1017/S0272263197001034](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0272263197001034)
  → **6 types de feedback** : recast, explicit correction, clarification request, metalinguistic, elicitation, repetition. Pilier de notre mapping tier → feedback.

- **Lyster, R. & Saito, K. (2010)**. "Oral Feedback in Classroom SLA: A Meta-Analysis". *SSLA* 32, 265-302.
  → **d = 1.16 pour prompts, d = 0.71 pour recasts** sur post-tests immédiats. Justifie "prompts > recasts" dans notre pédagogie.

- **Sheen, Y. (2007)**. "The Effect of Focused Written Corrective Feedback and Language Aptitude on ESL Learners' Acquisition of Articles". *TESOL Quarterly* 41(2), 255-283.
  → Effet plus fort chez apprenants à haute aptitude. Modulation individuelle.

- **Li, S. (2010)**. "The Effectiveness of Corrective Feedback in SLA: A Meta-Analysis". *Language Learning* 60(2), 309-365. [DOI 10.1111/j.1467-9922.2010.00561.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-9922.2010.00561.x)
  → 33 études ; d moyen 0.53-0.61. Explicit > implicit court terme ; similaires long terme.

- **Swain, M. (1985, 2005)**. Output Hypothesis.
  → Produire force **noticing the gap**, hypothesis testing, metalinguistic reflection. Justifie le pushed output après tier T3 `penalized` (B1+).

- **Truscott, J. (1996)**. "The Case Against Grammar Correction in L2 Writing Classes". *Language Learning* 46, 327-369.
  → Sur-correction écrite ne produit pas de gain, peut nuire. Justifie le `focused > unfocused` (1-2 structures cibles par session).

### Cognitive load et timing

- **Sweller, J., Ayres, P. & Kalyuga, S. (2011)**. *Cognitive Load Theory*. Springer.
  → Intrinsic/extraneous/germane load. Justifie le dosage par niveau.

- **Cowan, N. (2001)**. "The magical number 4 in short-term memory". *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 24, 87-114.
  → **Working memory ≈ 4 items** (révision du 7±2 de Miller). Max 3-4 corrections visibles simultanément.

- **Hattie, J. & Timperley, H. (2007)**. "The Power of Feedback". *Review of Educational Research* 77(1), 81-112.
  → **4 niveaux de feedback** (task, process, self-regulation, self). Les 3 premiers positifs, le 4e (self = "tu es intelligent") négatif. **d moyen feedback = 0.79**. Bannir praise d'intelligence.

- **Kluger, A. & DeNisi, A. (1996)**. "The effects of feedback interventions on performance". *Psychological Bulletin* 119(2), 254-284.
  → 38% des feedback interventions **dégradent** la performance quand centrées sur la personne.

- **Mueller, C. & Dweck, C. (1998)**. "Praise for intelligence can undermine children's motivation". *JPSP* 75(1), 33-52.
  → Praise d'intelligence → évitement de la difficulté. Banni.

- **Kulik, J. & Kulik, C.-L. (1988)**. "Timing of Feedback and Verbal Learning". *RER* 58(1), 79-97.
  → Immediate > delayed en labo, delayed > immediate en classroom (contre-intuitif). Pilier de notre timing inline vs end-of-session.

- **Shute, V. (2008)**. "Focus on Formative Feedback". *RER* 78(1), 153-189.
  → Immediate pour procedural, delayed pour transfer/retention. Débutants → immediate.

- **Cepeda, N. et al. (2006)**. "Distributed Practice in Verbal Recall Tasks: A Review and Quantitative Synthesis". *Psychological Bulletin* 132(3), 354-380.
  → Spacing ≈ 10-20% du retention interval visé. Base de nos intervalles J+1/J+3/J+7.

- **Roediger, H. & Karpicke, J. (2006)**. "Test-Enhanced Learning". *Psychological Science* 17(3), 249-255.
  → **Testing effect** : se tester > relire. Justifie spaced retrieval d'erreurs passées.

## 2. CEFR et Cambridge English Profile

- **Council of Europe (2020)**. *Common European Framework of Reference for Languages: Companion Volume*. [Site officiel](https://www.coe.int/en/web/common-european-framework-reference-languages)

- **Hawkins, J. A. & Filipović, L. (2012)**. *Criterial Features in L2 English: Specifying the Reference Levels of the CEFR*. CUP.
  → Livre fondateur. **1222 criterial features** identifiées. Base de notre tier T0 et des `criterial_level_emergence`/`criterial_level_mastery`.

- **O'Keeffe, A. & Mark, G. (2017)**. "The English Grammar Profile of learner competence". *Annual Review of Applied Linguistics*.
  → Validation NLP quantitative de l'EGP.

- **Cambridge English Profile (EGP + EVP)**. [Site officiel](https://englishprofile.org).

- **Pearson Global Scale of English (GSE)**. Échelle 10-90, 1800+ Can Do statements. [Site](https://www.pearson.com/languages/en-us/why-pearson/the-global-scale-of-english.html)

- **Hulstijn, J. (2015)**. *Language Proficiency in Native and Non-native Speakers: Theory and Research*. Benjamins.
  → Basic Language Cognition (BLC) vs Higher Language Cognition (HLC). Justifie matrices différentes oral vs écrit.

## 3. Modèles mathématiques et ML

### Item Response Theory

- **Samejima, F. (1969)**. *Estimation of Latent Ability Using a Response Pattern of Graded Scores*. Psychometric Monograph 17.
  → **Graded Response Model**. Remplace nos poids arbitraires 0.3/0.8 par seuils calibrés.

- **Linacre, J. M. (1989, ongoing)**. Many-Facet Rasch Measurement. [rasch.org/facet.htm](https://www.rasch.org/facet.htm)
  → Pour multi-rater (élève × item × LLM correcteur).

- **Birnbaum, A. (1968)**. 2PL IRT. In *Statistical Theories of Mental Test Scores* (Lord & Novick).

### Knowledge Tracing

- **Corbett, A. & Anderson, J. (1995)**. "Knowledge Tracing: Modeling the Acquisition of Procedural Knowledge". *User Modeling and User-Adapted Interaction* 4, 253-278.
  → **BKT**. HMM binaire, 4 paramètres par skill.

- **Pavlik, P., Cen, H. & Koedinger, K. (2009)**. "Performance Factors Analysis". *AIED 2009*.
  → PFA, régression logistique alternative.

- **Piech, C. et al. (2015)**. "Deep Knowledge Tracing". *NeurIPS*.
  → DKT (LSTM).

- **Ghosh, A., Heffernan, N. & Lan, A. (2020)**. "Context-Aware Attentive Knowledge Tracing". *KDD*.
  → **AKT** (attention + Rasch embedding). SOTA 2020-2024.

- **Yudelson, M., Koedinger, K. & Gordon, G. (2013)**. "Individualized Bayesian Knowledge Tracing Models". *EDM*.
  → BKT + individualization. Recommandé pour AcademIA.

### Spaced Repetition

- **Settles, B. & Meeder, B. (2016)**. "A Trainable Spaced Repetition Model for Language Learning". *ACL*. [PDF](https://research.duolingo.com/papers/settles.acl16.pdf)
  → **HLR (Half-Life Regression)**, l'algo Duolingo. +12% rétention vs Leitner.

- **Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler (FSRS-6, 2024-2025)**. [GitHub](https://github.com/open-spaced-repetition/fsrs4anki)
  → Meilleur algo open source actuel. DSR (difficulté, stability, retrievability) + 21 poids.

- **Lindsey, R. et al. (2014)**. "Improving Students' Long-Term Knowledge Retention Through Personalized Review". *Psychological Science* 25(3).
  → **DASH**.

- **Tabibian, B. et al. (2019)**. "Enhancing human learning via spaced repetition optimization". *PNAS* 116(10).
  → **MEMORIZE** (marked temporal point process).

### Bayesian & autres

- **Bingham, E. et al. (2019)**. *Pyro: Deep Universal Probabilistic Programming*. → NumPyro.
- **Carpenter, B. et al. (2017)**. *Stan: A Probabilistic Programming Language*.
- **girth (eribean)** Python lib IRT. [GitHub](https://github.com/eribean/girth)
- **py-irt (nd-ball)** Scalable IRT Python. [arxiv 2203.01282](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.01282)
- **pyBKT (CAHLR)** BKT Python. [GitHub](https://github.com/CAHLR/pyBKT)
- **lifelines** Python survival analysis. Cox PH pour half-life d'erreur.
- **pymer4** Python wrapper lme4 pour GLMM.

## 5. Annotation d'erreur

- **Nicholls, D. (2003)**. "The Cambridge Learner Corpus — error coding and analysis for lexicography and ELT". [PDF](https://ucrel.lancs.ac.uk/publications/CL2003/papers/nicholls.pdf)
  → Schéma CLC : ~88 codes, bi-lettre (type × word class).

- **Bryant, C., Felice, M., Andersen, Ø. & Briscoe, T. (2019)**. "The BEA-2019 Shared Task on Grammatical Error Correction". [Paper PDF](https://chrisjbryant.github.io/assets/pdf/bea2019st.pdf) [Site](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/research/nl/bea2019st/)
  → Introduit **ERRANT**, standard NLP de facto pour annotation GEC.

- **Bryant, C. et al. (2023)**. "Grammatical Error Correction: A Survey of the State of the Art". *Computational Linguistics* 49(3), 643-701.

## 6. LLM as grader / tutor (2023-2026)

### LLM-as-judge généraliste

- **Zheng, L. et al. (2023)**. "Judging LLM-as-a-Judge with MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena". *NeurIPS*. [arxiv 2306.05685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.05685)
  → GPT-4 atteint >80% accord avec préférences humaines. Fonde la pratique.

- **Liu, Y. et al. (2023)**. "G-Eval: NLG Evaluation using GPT-4 with Better Human Alignment". *EMNLP*. [arxiv 2303.16634](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.16634)
  → Chain-of-thought scoring. Transposable au grading L2 via rubric CECRL.

- **"LLMs-as-Judges: A Comprehensive Survey" (2024)**. [arxiv 2412.05579](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.05579)
  → 12 biais identifiés, mitigations.

### LLM × L2 spécifique

- **Yancey, K. P. et al. (2023)**. "Rating Short L2 Essays on the CEFR Scale with GPT-4". *BEA*. [ACL](https://aclanthology.org/2023.bea-1.49/)
  → Few-shot + rubric → GPT-4 ≈ AES modernes. **Biais L1-dépendant documenté**.

- **Bannò, S. et al. (2024)**. "Can GPT-4 do L2 analytic assessment?". *BEA*. [ACL](https://aclanthology.org/2024.bea-1.14/) [arxiv 2404.18557](https://arxiv.org/html/2404.18557v1)
  → Pearson 0.866, Spearman 0.874 sur W&I, 9 traits CECRL.

- **Pfau, J., Polio, C. & Xu, Y. (2023)**. "Exploring the potential of ChatGPT in assessing L2 writing accuracy". *Applied Linguistics and TESOL*.
  → **99% précision error ID mais recall faible**. Fonde notre besoin de rules layer.

- **Fang, T. et al. (2023)**. "Is ChatGPT a Highly Fluent Grammatical Error Correction System?". [arxiv 2304.01746](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.01746)
  → **Overcorrection systématique**. Fonde le rules-layer comme garde-fou.

- **Pak, E. et al. (2025)**. "Alignment Drift in CEFR-prompted LLMs for Interactive Spanish Tutoring". *BEA 2025*. [arxiv 2505.08351](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.08351)
  → **Drift après 5-9 tours** de dialogue CEFR-prompted. Fonde notre anti-drift (re-injection periodic).

### Prompting

- **Wei, J. et al. (2022)**. "Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning". [arxiv 2201.11903](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903)

- **Wang, X. et al. (2022)**. "Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought". [arxiv 2203.11171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171)
  → n-sampling + médian. Base de notre stratégie self-consistency ciblée.

- **Madaan, A. et al. (2023)**. "Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback". [arxiv 2303.17651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651)

- **Bai, Y. et al. (2022)**. "Constitutional AI". [arxiv 2212.08073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073)

- **LLM-RUBRIC (ACL 2024)**. [aclanthology 2024.acl-long.745](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.745.pdf)
  → Rubric multi-dim réduit length bias.

### Cross-lingual

- **Littell, P. et al. (2017)**. "URIEL and lang2vec: Representing languages as typological, geographical, and phylogenetic vectors". *EACL*. [GitHub](https://github.com/antonisa/lang2vec)
  → Base de nos priors typologiques L1-L2 transfer.

- **Qwen 2.5 Technical Report (2024)**. [arxiv 2412.15115](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.15115)
  → Multilingue 29+ langues, candidat self-hosting futur.

## 8. Standards par langue

### Langues

- **English Grammar Profile (EGP)** — 1222 criterial features. [englishprofile.org](https://englishprofile.org/english-grammar/)
- **English Vocabulary Profile (EVP)** — ~7000 mots/sens × CEFR. [englishprofile.org](https://englishprofile.org/?menu=evp-online)
- **Plan Curricular del Instituto Cervantes (PCIC)** — espagnol. [cvc.cervantes.es](https://cvc.cervantes.es/ensenanza/biblioteca_ele/plan_curricular/indice.htm)
- **Profile Deutsch (Glaboniat et al. 2005)** — allemand. Langenscheidt.
- **Sillabo CILS (Università per Stranieri di Siena)** — italien.
- **JF Standard (Japan Foundation)** — japonais, CEFR-aligné.
- **JLPT communautaires** (N5-N1). [jlptsensei.com](https://jlptsensei.com/)

### Non-langues (pour domaines futurs)
